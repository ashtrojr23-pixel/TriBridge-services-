What does LGBTQ+ stand for? ‚ñº
LGBTQ+ stands for Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, Transgender, Queer (or Questioning), and + represents many other identities including Intersex, Asexual, Pansexual, Non-binary, and more. It is an umbrella term for the diverse community of people whose gender identity or sexual orientation differs from the traditional heterosexual or cisgender norm.
What is gender identity vs sexual orientation? ‚ñº
Gender identity is your internal sense of your own gender ‚Äî man, woman, non-binary, or another identity. Sexual orientation is about who you are attracted to romantically or sexually. These are two completely separate things. For example, a transgender woman can be heterosexual, lesbian, bisexual, or any other orientation.
What does it mean to be non-binary? ‚ñº
Non-binary is a term for gender identities that don't fit exclusively into "man" or "woman." Non-binary people may identify as both, neither, or somewhere between. Many use they/them pronouns, though this varies individually. Non-binary is a valid, natural gender identity.
Is being LGBTQ+ a choice? ‚ñº
No. Sexual orientation and gender identity are not choices. Every major psychological and medical organisation ‚Äî including the American Psychological Association and the World Health Organisation ‚Äî affirms that being LGBTQ+ is a natural variation of human identity. "Conversion therapy" is widely condemned as harmful and ineffective.
How can I be a better ally? ‚ñº
Being a good ally means: listening without judgment, using correct names and pronouns, speaking up against discrimination, educating yourself, not outing someone without consent, and making your spaces actively welcoming. Allyship is an ongoing practice that shows in everyday actions.
